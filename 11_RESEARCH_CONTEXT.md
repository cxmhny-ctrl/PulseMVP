# Pulse Research Context

## Workflow Summary

The product originated from a research and validation workflow.

### Phase 1: Market Research
Initial Instagram hashtag search via Composio was not useful because Instagram Graph API limitations prevent broad public hashtag search in the desired way. Research pivoted to Reddit and broader web search.

Relevant communities and sources included:

- r/AppIdeas
- r/SomebodyMakeThis
- r/Startup_Ideas
- r/Lightbulb
- ADHD productivity complaints
- ADHD task-initiation pain posts
- “I wish someone would build” style threads

16 market gap signals were found.

### Phase 2: Idea Roasting
A DeepSeek-style ruthless validator reviewed the ideas. 15 ideas were killed. One survived:

```text
ADHD Task Companion — AI breaks tasks into micro-steps with adaptive, energy-aware reminders for executive dysfunction.
```

Score: 8/10.

### Phase 3: Swarm Prediction
A simulated focus group of ADHD users and a therapist reviewed the idea.

Findings:

- Problem-solution fit: 7/10
- Willingness to pay: 4/10
- Retention risk: high
- Overall: too early to tell

Fatal flaw:

```text
ADHD users have deep app fatigue and skepticism from past disappointments. The free ChatGPT workaround weakens the pricing story.
```

Pricing verdict:

```text
$6.99/month was considered overpriced.
```

### Phase 4: Perfect App Design Session
A second simulation asked what the app would look like if it were perfect.

The group converged on Pulse:

```text
A proactive ADHD stuck-breaking system that reaches the user through widgets, SMS, notifications, and voice, instead of requiring the user to open an app.
```

## Key Product Conclusions

### The App Must Reach the User
The biggest insight was that ADHD users often cannot be expected to open another app when stuck. Pulse should therefore live in:

- widgets
- SMS
- notifications
- voice
- family/professional support surfaces

### The Main Enemy Is App Fatigue
Pulse must avoid feeling like another productivity system to maintain.

### The Chatbot Is Not the Moat
Generic AI can already break down tasks. Pulse must win through:

- timing
- context
- ambient nudging
- frictionless engagement
- personalization
- trust

### Pricing Should Avoid Subscription-Only Positioning
The research rejected subscription-only pricing. One-time purchase should be the public positioning, but cloud-heavy features such as SMS and AI may need fair-use limits or optional usage packs.

## Recommended Research Questions for Beta

1. Does Pulse help users start tasks more often than reminders?
2. Which channel works best: widget, notification, SMS, or voice?
3. What nudge frequency feels helpful versus annoying?
4. Do users trust the detection language?
5. Does the product feel different enough from ChatGPT?
6. Are users willing to pay one-time?
7. Would families use shared profiles without conflict?
8. Would therapists recommend it to clients?
9. What false positives feel acceptable?
10. Does weekly summary increase retention?
